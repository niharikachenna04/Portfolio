AGI, Superintelligence, and Social Fragmentation

Forbes | Lance B. Eliot | October 12, 2025

Article Link

Original Source

Article: The Bold Claim That AGI And AI Superintelligence Will Radically Fragment Society

Source: Forbes

View Original Article

Article Focus

This article explains how advanced AI could divide society instead of bringing people together.

The article focuses on AGI and artificial superintelligence, warning that people may trust different AI systems as authorities and form conflicting beliefs, decisions, and social groups.

Summary

Summary of the Article

The article argues that Artificial General Intelligence and Artificial Superintelligence may not automatically create unity or agreement. Instead, advanced AI could cause society to become more fragmented if different people, organizations, or governments follow different AI systems.

A key concern is that people may treat powerful AI as an ultimate authority. If one AI system gives one recommendation and another AI system gives a different recommendation, groups may disagree about which system is correct. This could create political, cultural, economic, and ethical divisions.

The article also explains that the risk is not only the technology itself, but how humans respond to it. Governance, trust, transparency, and responsible use will be important if advanced AI becomes part of major decisions.

Important Takeaways

Key Ideas from the Article

AI as Authority

People may begin to trust AI systems as decision-making authorities, especially if those systems appear smarter, faster, or more objective than humans.

Different AI Systems

Different groups may follow different AI platforms, creating disagreement about which system should guide decisions, standards, and priorities.

Social Fragmentation

Instead of creating agreement, advanced AI could increase polarization by reinforcing separate beliefs, values, and interpretations.

Need for Governance

The article shows that AI progress must be supported by ethical rules, legal responsibility, transparency, and human oversight.

AEC Relevance

Connection to the AEC Industry

This article connects to the AEC industry because advanced AI may influence design decisions, structural analysis, cost modeling, scheduling, safety planning, and project coordination.

If different firms use different AI systems, the construction industry could face conflicting design standards, compatibility problems, and questions about who is responsible when AI-supported decisions lead to errors. This makes human review and professional accountability very important.
