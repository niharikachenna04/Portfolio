The Future Risk of AGI and AI Superintelligence

Forbes article by Lance B. Eliot | October 12, 2025

View Source
Selected Source

Article Information

Title: The Bold Claim That AGI And AI Superintelligence Will Radically Fragment Society Source: Forbes Author: Lance B. Eliot Date: October 12, 2025 Format: Online news analysis / opinion article Topic: AGI, AI Superintelligence, society, and ethics

This source is credible because Forbes is a recognized business and technology publication, and the author focuses on AI ethics and advanced AI systems.

Summary

Main Idea

The article argues that Artificial General Intelligence and Artificial Superintelligence may not automatically unite society. Instead, these advanced AI systems could create new divisions if different people, organizations, or governments begin trusting different AI systems as sources of authority.

A major concern is that people may treat advanced AI like an “oracle” or final decision-maker. If one group trusts one AI system and another group trusts a different system, society could become divided over which AI output is correct, who controls the system, and how much power AI should have in decision-making.

Key Insight

The real challenge is not only advanced AI itself, but how humans choose to trust, control, and use it.

Without strong governance, transparency, and human judgment, AGI and ASI could increase conflict instead of creating shared progress.

Main Findings

Important Ideas

AI as Authority

People may begin relying on advanced AI systems for major decisions in law, medicine, policy, business, and daily life.

Competing AI Systems

Different groups may align with different AI systems, creating disagreement over which system is more accurate or trustworthy.

Social Fragmentation

Instead of creating consensus, advanced AI could amplify polarization and create ideological divides.

Control and Power

Whoever controls advanced AI systems may gain significant influence over decisions, knowledge, and economic power.

Broader Implications

Impact on Society

The article explains that AGI and ASI could create political fragmentation if governments compete for AI dominance. It could also lead to economic disruption if automation benefits are unevenly distributed across workers, firms, and countries.

Another important concern is the shift in authority. People may begin deferring important legal, medical, business, or policy decisions to AI systems. This creates questions about accountability, fairness, bias, and trust.

The article suggests that social trust, ethical frameworks, and governance systems will be essential. The main risk is not only technological power, but how humans respond to that power.

AEC Relevance

Why This Matters in Construction

In the AEC industry, advanced AI could support design optimization, structural analysis, cost modeling, scheduling, risk review, and safety prediction. However, the article raises important questions about whether firms should fully trust AI-generated recommendations over human engineers and project professionals.

If different firms adopt different AI platforms, the industry may face fragmentation through competing design standards, conflicting workflows, interoperability issues, and different project methodologies.

The workforce may also change. Project managers, architects, and engineers may shift from being direct decision-makers to becoming AI supervisors who validate outputs, apply professional judgment, and manage ethical responsibility.

Reflection

AI should support professionals, not replace responsible decision-making.

This article helped me understand that the future of AI in AEC is not only about productivity. It is also about trust, responsibility, legal accountability, and ethical decision-making.
