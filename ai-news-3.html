The Future Risk of AGI and AI Superintelligence

This page reviews a Forbes article by Lance B. Eliot that discusses how AGI and AI superintelligence may not simply improve society, but could also create new divisions, power struggles, and ethical challenges.

View Source Back to Weekly Articles
Selected Source

Article Information

Title: AGI and AI Superintelligence Source: Forbes Author: Lance B. Eliot Date: October 12, 2025 Format: Online news analysis Topic: AI ethics and society
Summary

Main idea of the article

The article argues that AGI and AI superintelligence may not automatically bring society together. Instead, powerful AI systems could increase social fragmentation if different groups trust different AI systems, outputs, or interpretations.

The article suggests that people may begin treating advanced AI like an authority or “oracle.” This could create disagreement over which AI system is correct, who controls the technology, and how much power AI should have in human decision-making.

Core Argument

The biggest risk may not be AI itself, but how humans respond to its power.

If people, companies, and governments disagree over AI authority, advanced AI could deepen division instead of creating shared progress.

Key Arguments

Why AGI could fragment society

AI as authority

People may begin trusting AI systems as final decision-makers for complex questions in policy, law, medicine, business, and daily life.

Competing AI systems

Different groups may follow different AI tools, creating disagreement over which system is more accurate, fair, or trustworthy.

Ideological divides

AI systems could influence political, cultural, or social beliefs, creating divisions similar to competing ideologies.

Control and power

Organizations that control advanced AI systems may gain major influence over knowledge, decisions, and economic power.

Society Impact

Advanced AI could reshape trust, power, and decision-making.

The article highlights risks such as political fragmentation, economic disruption, authority shifts, and cultural division.

Broader Implications

What this means for society

If AGI or AI superintelligence becomes widely used, governments may compete for AI dominance, companies may depend more heavily on AI systems, and people may disagree about how much influence AI should have.

The article shows that governance, ethics, transparency, and trust will become just as important as the technology itself.

AEC Relevance

Why this matters to Architecture, Engineering, and Construction

Design decisions

If AI begins optimizing design, cost, scheduling, and structural analysis, firms must decide how much authority to give AI-generated recommendations.

Legal responsibility

If an AI-generated design or schedule creates an error, the industry must determine who is responsible: the designer, contractor, software provider, or project team.

Industry fragmentation

Different companies may adopt different AI platforms, creating conflicts in standards, workflows, formats, and interoperability.

Workforce change

Project managers, engineers, and architects may shift from doing all analysis manually to supervising and validating AI-generated outputs.

AEC Takeaway

AI can improve construction productivity, but it also raises questions about control, responsibility, and trust.

For the AEC industry, the challenge is not only adopting AI tools, but also creating ethical, legal, and organizational systems that guide how those tools are used.

My Reflection

What I learned from this source

This article helped me understand that future AI development is not only a technical issue. It is also a social and professional issue. In construction, AI may help with scheduling, estimating, safety prediction, design coordination, and documentation.

However, the industry must still ask important questions: Who checks the AI output? Who is responsible if the AI is wrong? How do small firms compete if advanced AI tools are controlled by large companies? These questions make AI governance very important for the future of AEC.
