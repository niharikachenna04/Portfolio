The Future Risk of AGI and AI Superintelligence

This page reviews a Forbes article that discusses how advanced AI systems like AGI and superintelligence may not unify society, but instead create new divisions, challenges, and ethical concerns.

View Source Back to Weekly Articles
Selected Source

Article Information

Title: AGI & Superintelligence Source: Forbes Author: Lance B. Eliot Date: October 2025 Format: AI News Article Topic: AI & Society
Summary

Main Idea

The article explains that powerful AI systems like AGI and ASI may not bring people together. Instead, they could create divisions because different groups may trust different AI systems.

People may treat AI as a decision authority, leading to conflicts over which system is correct and who controls it.

Key Insight

The real challenge is not AI itself, but how humans choose to use it.

Without proper control and trust, AI could increase social and professional conflicts.

Key Points

Important Ideas

AI Authority

People may depend on AI for important decisions.

Different Systems

Groups may follow different AI outputs.

Division

AI could increase social and professional disagreements.

Power Control

Organizations controlling AI may gain influence.

AEC Relevance

Why it matters in Construction

In the AEC industry, AI can support design, scheduling, and cost estimation. However, it also raises questions about responsibility, decision-making, and trust.

Different firms using different AI tools may lead to conflicts in standards and workflows.

Reflection

AI should support professionals, not replace decision-making.

Understanding AI limitations is just as important as using it.
