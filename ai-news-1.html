Article Focus

Summary

Summary of the Article

The article explains that 2026 represents a major shift in how Artificial Intelligence is used in construction. Previously, AI was mostly limited to experimental applications and basic automation. However, it is now becoming a core part of construction workflows, supporting real project execution.

A key development is the emergence of Agentic AI. These systems go beyond simple responses and can actively reason, plan, and complete tasks. In construction, this includes scheduling, document analysis, coordination, and risk evaluation.

Another major advancement is the use of visual and spatial data. AI can analyze site images, scans, and 3D models to compare real-world progress with design intent, improving tracking accuracy and decision-making.

Overall, AI is transitioning from a supporting tool into an integrated system that improves efficiency, reduces manual work, and enhances project outcomes.
