Academic Background

From architecture to construction management

My academic background began in architecture, where I learned to think through design, drawings, spatial organization, and visual communication. That foundation helped me understand how ideas move from concept to built form.

As I continued learning, I became more interested in how projects are planned, coordinated, documented, and executed on site. This led me toward construction management and gave me a broader view of the built environment.

My Perspective

Architecture helps me see construction as both a design process and an execution process.

I look at projects through both visual and practical lenses. Design thinking helps me understand intent, while construction management helps me focus on coordination, schedule, communication, and real project delivery.

AI Learning

Using AI for practical construction tasks

Through this course, I explored how AI can support real construction management activities instead of only being seen as a general technology trend.

The areas that stood out to me most were documentation, estimating, project coordination, and communication. These are tasks where AI can help organize information, summarize content, compare options, and reduce repetitive work when used carefully.

Career Goals

Where I want to grow

My goal is to grow in construction management with a focus on project coordination, execution, and practical technology use.

I want to continue building skills that help project teams communicate clearly, manage information effectively, and use tools like AI in a responsible and useful way.
