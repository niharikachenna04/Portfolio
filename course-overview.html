AI IN AEC INDUSTRY
Section 02

Course Overview

This course introduced how artificial intelligence is being used in the architecture, engineering, construction, and facilities management fields. It focused on AI concepts, real industry applications, responsible use, and how these tools can support future AEC workflows.

Course Focus

Understanding AI in the AEC Industry

The course helped me understand AI as more than just a technology trend. I learned how AI can support construction planning, documentation, communication, project organization, and decision-making in the built environment.

Course Approach

Connecting classroom learning with real industry practice.

Through readings, discussions, article reviews, and project work, the course showed how AI tools can be applied to real AEC tasks while still requiring human judgment, accuracy checks, and responsible use.

Main Topics

What the Course Covered

These topics helped build a foundation for understanding how AI connects to design, construction, project management, and facility-related work.

AI Concepts and Terminology

Basic AI language, current trends, common tool categories, and how AI is discussed within the built environment.

Industry Applications

Practical examples such as project planning, document review, scheduling support, site progress tracking, and facilities management.

Responsible Use

The importance of checking AI outputs, understanding limitations, avoiding overreliance, and using human judgment.

Course Projects

Assignments and portfolio work that connected AI ideas to real AEC workflows and professional practice.

Learning Outcomes

What I Learned

The most useful part of the course was learning how to evaluate AI as a support tool, not as a replacement for professional thinking.

Identify AI Use Cases

I can recognize where AI may be useful in design coordination, construction workflows, documentation, and project communication.

Evaluate Benefits and Limits

I learned that AI can improve speed and organization, but its results still need review, context, and verification.

Why This Course Matters

Academic Value

This course is valuable because AI is becoming an important part of the AEC industry. It helped me understand AI in a practical way, connected to coursework, professional skills, and future construction workflows.
