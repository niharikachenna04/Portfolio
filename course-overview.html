Course Overview

This section introduces the purpose of the course and the major ideas explored throughout the semester.

Course Title

CE 895: AI in the AEC Industry

This course focuses on how Artificial Intelligence is currently being used in the architecture, engineering, and construction industry, where it is headed, and how it may affect different construction roles and project workflows.

Course Focus

Understanding AI as a practical tool for construction workflows, not only as a future trend.

The course connected AI concepts with documentation, estimating, planning, coordination, communication, and decision-making in the built environment.

Course Description

What the course covers

The course combines conceptual understanding with practical exploration of AI tools applied to real construction management tasks.

AI Concepts

Introduces the growing role of AI in planning, documentation, estimating, analysis, communication, and project decision-making.

Construction Applications

Explores how AI can support construction workflows by improving efficiency, organizing information, and helping teams make better decisions.

Course Objectives

Main learning goals

Understand AI in AEC

Understand the role of AI in architecture, engineering, construction, and facilities management.

Analyze Workflow Impact

Analyze how AI may affect traditional construction workflows, roles, and responsibilities.

Apply AI Tools

Use AI tools for tasks such as documentation, estimating, summarization, and analysis.

Explore Task-Specific Tools

Identify how different AI tools can support specific construction management activities.

Evaluate AI Responsibly

Recognize ethical, practical, and professional considerations related to AI adoption.

Key Themes

Topics explored in the course

The course highlights how AI supports productivity, coordination, communication, and decision-making across the built environment.

It also examines the opportunities and limitations of AI use in construction practice, including where human judgment remains important.

Course Value

Why this course matters

This course helped me build a stronger understanding of how emerging technologies can connect with real construction workflows and industry needs.
