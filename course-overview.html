AI IN AEC INDUSTRY
Section 02

Course Overview

This course introduced how artificial intelligence is being used and studied in the architecture, engineering, construction, and facilities fields. It focused on AI concepts, current industry examples, and practical applications connected to the built environment.

Course Focus

Understanding AI as an emerging AEC topic

The course focused on understanding AI as an emerging topic in the AEC industry. It explained how AI can support project-related work, organize information, and influence future professional practice.

It also introduced the importance of evaluating AI carefully by considering both the benefits of AI tools and the limits that require human judgment, accuracy checks, and responsible use.

Course Approach

Connecting academic concepts with real industry examples.

Through readings, discussions, assignments, and tool exploration, students studied how AI is being applied in different parts of the built environment.

This approach helped connect theory with practice and showed how AI topics relate to construction tasks, project information, and current industry changes.

What the Course Covers

Main course topics

AI Concepts in AEC

The course covered key AI ideas, terminology, current trends, and examples of digital tools used in architecture, engineering, construction, and facilities management.

Industry Use Cases

The course explored practical examples such as project planning, document review, cost-related tasks, site progress tracking, and facility operations.

Main Learning Goals

Course outcomes

Understand AI Terminology

Learn the basic language, concepts, and categories used when discussing AI in the built environment.

Identify Industry Applications

Recognize where AI is being applied across design, construction, project management, and facility-related work.

Evaluate Usefulness and Limits

Study the strengths, risks, and limitations of AI tools, including the need for human review and responsible use.

Apply Course Topics

Use assignments, article reviews, and project-based activities to connect course ideas with AEC practice.

Why This Course Matters

Academic value

This course is valuable because AI is becoming an important topic in the built environment. It helps students understand AI beyond general trends and study it as a subject connected to industry practice, professional skills, and future construction workflows.
