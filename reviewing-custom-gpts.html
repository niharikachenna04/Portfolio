Project 02

Reviewing Custom GPTs

This project focused on exploring how custom-built GPT tools can support users in different tasks. I reviewed one general-purpose GPT and one AEC-related GPT to understand how these tools work, where they are useful, and what limitations they may have.

Project Purpose

Why I reviewed these GPTs

The goal of this assignment was to understand how Custom GPTs are created for specific purposes and how users interact with them through real prompts. Instead of only reading about AI tools, I tested them directly and evaluated how well they responded.

I selected a Career Coach GPT as a general-purpose tool and an MS Project GPT as an AEC-related tool. This allowed me to compare how a GPT performs in a broad guidance task versus a more technical project management task.

Featured GPT

Career Coach GPT

I used the Career Coach GPT to get support with career planning, resume preparation, job readiness, and professional skill development. The responses were simple, clear, and helpful for someone preparing for future career opportunities.

AEC GPT

MS Project GPT

I used the MS Project GPT to understand project scheduling concepts. It explained how to organize tasks, set durations, connect activities, understand dependencies, and recognize milestones in a project schedule.

Comparison

What I learned from both tools

Career Coach GPT

This GPT worked well for general career guidance. It helped me think about resumes, job preparation, skill improvement, and professional growth.

MS Project GPT

This GPT worked well for explaining scheduling concepts. It made project planning easier to understand by organizing tasks logically and explaining milestones.

Main Difference

The Career Coach GPT was broader and advice-based, while the MS Project GPT was more focused on a technical AEC-related workflow.

What Worked Well

Strengths I noticed

Both GPTs gave clear and easy-to-understand responses. They helped organize information quickly and responded well when I asked follow-up questions. The Career Coach GPT was helpful for career direction, while the MS Project GPT made scheduling concepts easier to understand.

What Could Improve

Limitations I noticed

Some responses were a little general. The Career Coach GPT could improve by asking more questions about the user's background before giving advice. The MS Project GPT could be stronger if it gave more step-by-step guidance for using Microsoft Project software.

AEC Relevance

Why this matters for construction management

Custom GPTs can be valuable in the AEC industry because construction work involves many repeated tasks, technical explanations, planning steps, and communication needs. A focused GPT can help users understand scheduling, organize project information, prepare documents, and learn software workflows.

For students and entry-level professionals, this type of tool can make complex topics easier to approach. Instead of struggling with a blank page or confusing software concepts, users can ask questions and receive structured guidance.

Final Reflection

Reviewing these GPTs helped me understand that custom AI tools are most useful when they are focused, clear, and connected to a real task.

Overall, I think Custom GPTs are helpful because they give quick support in a specific area. They may not replace human judgment or detailed learning, but they can make the first step easier by explaining ideas, organizing information, and helping users move forward with more confidence.
