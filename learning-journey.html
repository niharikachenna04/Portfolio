Starting Point

Initial curiosity and uncertainty

At the beginning of the semester, AI felt exciting but broad. I was interested in its potential, but I was still figuring out how it connected in a practical way to construction and facilities management.

Mid-Semester

From general awareness to applied understanding

As the coursework progressed, I began to see clearer links between AI and real workflows such as communication, documentation, analysis, and operational support. The topic became more concrete and professionally relevant.

Key Shift

Learning to evaluate, not just admire

One of the most important lessons was that AI should not simply be treated as impressive technology. It needs to be assessed carefully for usefulness, accuracy, responsibility, and fit within actual project or facilities contexts.

End of Course

A more grounded and future-focused perspective

By the end of the semester, I saw AI less as a trend and more as a set of tools and ideas that may shape future industry practice—especially when combined with strong judgment, communication, and domain knowledge.

Reflection Themes

What I learned most

AI needs context

Technology becomes more meaningful when tied to real industry problems instead of abstract claims.

Communication matters

A strong idea becomes much more effective when it is explained clearly through visuals, writing, and structure.

Critical thinking is essential

It is important to ask not only what AI can do, but what it should do and where human oversight remains important.

Challenges

What challenged me

  • Understanding fast-changing AI terminology and tools
  • Separating realistic industry value from hype
  • Turning broad topics into focused and meaningful assignments
  • Balancing critical evaluation with creativity and experimentation
Looking Ahead

What I want to keep exploring

  • AI use cases for project teams and coordination workflows
  • Facilities and operations applications supported by data
  • Better ways to present technical ideas visually and clearly
  • The future relationship between human expertise and intelligent tools
Final Reflection

This course helped me think about AI with more structure, more realism, and more relevance to my future in the built environment.
